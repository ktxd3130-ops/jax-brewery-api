Pint — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 20, 2026

Pint ("the app," "we") is a brewery-finder app for craft-beer fans 21+. This policy explains what data the app uses and how. Our guiding principle: Pint collects as little as possible and exists only to help you find a good brewery.

The short version

  • No account. No sign-up. We don't ask for your name, email, or password.
  • We don't sell your data, show ads, or track you across apps or websites.
  • Your location is used to find breweries near you — and nothing else.

Location

If you grant location permission, the app uses your device's approximate or precise location to find and rank breweries near you and to estimate distance. Your coordinates are sent to our own backend API (hosted on Vercel) at the moment of a search so it can compute nearby results. We do not store your location history, build a profile, or associate your location with your identity. If you deny location access, Pint falls back to a curated local list and remains fully usable.

Information we do not collect

We do not collect your name, email address, contacts, photos, advertising identifiers, or browsing activity. Pint has no user accounts and runs no third-party advertising or cross-app tracking SDKs.

Third-party services

To show brewery details, Pint's backend may request data from third parties on your behalf. Brewery names, hours, and content come from our curated dataset and from these services:

  • Google Places — to display reviews, photos, and venue details (called by our backend; subject to Google's Privacy Policy).
  • Open Brewery DB — to list breweries when you browse cities outside our curated area.
  • Weather data — to make weather-aware recommendations.
  • OpenStreetMap — map tiles for the map view.
  • Vercel — our hosting provider, which may process standard server logs (such as IP address) to deliver and secure the service.

Data retention

We do not maintain user profiles. Search requests are processed to return results and are not retained as personal records. Standard, short-lived server logs may exist for security and reliability.

Children

Pint concerns alcohol and is intended for adults of legal drinking age. It is not directed to children and we do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 17.

Your choices

You can revoke location access at any time in your device Settings. Doing so does not break the app — it falls back to a curated list.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update the date above and post the revised version at this URL. If we ever add analytics, push notifications, or accounts, we'll update this policy and the App Store privacy details before doing so.
